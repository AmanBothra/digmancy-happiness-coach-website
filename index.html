02. The method, in four movements

From fear to freedom.
Hierarchy to humanity.

The practice is not a framework you memorise. It is four small movements, rehearsed together, until the leader who walks into Wednesday's meeting is a different kind of listener than the one who walked in on Monday.

i.
rehumanise the room

Before we fix, we describe. Name the ache — the exact Tuesday-evening feeling — precisely enough that the reader recognises themselves.

ii.
voice before answer

Silence is not a personality trait; it is a survival pattern. We practice putting words on what has been wordless.

iii.
presence over pressure

Power is not lost through truth. It is built through a kind of emotional authority most leadership books never teach.

iv.
circle not stage

Voice is reclaimed by practice, in safe rooms, with other leaders who are tired of performing strength they do not always feel.

Her motto, and ours: Happiness is a choice.

04. The practice, and the person behind it

Meet Aastha
Tatia.

Aastha is a Happiness Coach for Leaders — a quiet, specific voice of authenticity and consciousness in the Indian leadership space.

Her work, in her own words, begins with a simple conviction: every human being deserves to own their voice, and to express who they truly are — unapologetically, uninhibitedly, and responsibly. Happiness, she teaches, is not something we chase. It is something we uncover when we finally stop performing.

She founded the Authentic Leadership Circle to help leaders lead boldly, speak freely, and live in alignment — the leaders who have achieved a great deal on paper, and still crave something deeper. The practice is small, intimate, and deliberately unhurried.
